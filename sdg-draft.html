Sustainable Development Goal (SDG) Mapping

This page outlines how our solar company contributes to the UN Sustainable Development Goals through clean energy expansion, climate resilience, innovation, responsible production, and economic upliftment.

SDG Icons

SDG 7 – Affordable & Clean Energy

  • Providing high-efficiency solar panels to households, businesses, and rural communities.
  • Reducing energy dependency on fossil fuels and lowering monthly utility bills.
  • Deploying rooftop, ground-mounted, and off-grid microgrid solar systems.
  • KPI: Total renewable energy generated (kWh) yearly.
  • KPI: Number of low-income households getting solar access.

SDG 8 – Decent Work & Economic Growth

  • Creating green jobs through the manufacturing, installation, and maintenance of solar systems.
  • Supporting skill development programs in solar technology for local technicians and youth.
  • Promoting fair wages, safe working conditions, and equal employment opportunities.
  • Encouraging sustainable economic growth by reducing energy costs for businesses.
  • KPI: Number of green jobs created annually.
  • KPI: Workforce trained and certified in solar technologies.

SDG 9 – Industry, Innovation & Infrastructure

  • Developing reliable solar infrastructure for sustainable industrial growth.
  • Using advanced solar cell technology and energy storage integration.
  • Promoting local component manufacturing to reduce transportation emissions.
  • KPI: Panel efficiency improvement rate yearly.

SDG 11 – Sustainable Cities & Communities

  • Powering public buildings, streetlights, schools, and community centers with solar energy.
  • Supporting disaster-resilient solar microgrids for emergency backup.
  • KPI: Percentage of community infrastructure using clean energy.

SDG 12 – Responsible Consumption & Production

  • Using sustainable raw materials and reducing waste in production.
  • Implementing a solar panel recycling and take-back program.
  • Encouraging energy-efficient inverters and long-life solar modules.
  • KPI: Percentage of materials recycled per year.

SDG 13 – Climate Action

  • Significantly reducing carbon emissions through expanded solar adoption.
  • Promoting climate-resilient solar installations in vulnerable areas.
  • Tracking lifecycle carbon reduction performance.
  • KPI: Tons of CO₂ emissions avoided annually.

SDG 17 – Partnerships for the Goals

  • Working with government, NGOs, and investors to expand renewable energy adoption.
  • Collaborating with universities for research and innovation in solar technology.
  • KPI: Number of active partnerships and joint sustainability projects.
